# AI Provenance Log - article on spec-driven AI methodology

This document distills 55 process journal entries (2026-07-04 - 2026-07-13) of the creation of a scoping-review-type scientific article on spec-driven AI methodology (FSAIM) into a coherent AI-participation provenance log. This is layer L6 of the SDD methodology applied to the article's own process (dogfooding) - the article postulates auditability of decisions and AI participation, and this log is evidence that this postulate is fulfilled by its own creation process. The scope covers all phases: from feasibility analysis and corpus construction, through coding and chapter writing, to editing, submission to arXiv/SSRN, correspondence with editors of the Journal of Economic Surveys, and cross-model coding validation (v2 version for a JES special issue).

## 1. AI models and tools

- **Claude Science agent** - main executor of the entire process: retrieval and corpus construction, primary coding (28, then 41 papers), writing all chapters, PL/EN narrative editing, building objects (figures, tables), preparing submission packages (arXiv, SSRN, repo, JES), working correspondence, cross-model validation infrastructure.
- **Model from the GPT family** (D4: "the latest version at the time coding started", labeled in logs as GPT 5.6 Terra) - second coder in cross-model validation B2: coded 28 full-text papers without access to Claude's primary codes, on the author's account.
- **Reasoning model** (unnamed version in the logs) - three roles: (a) semantic judge assessing equivalence of free-text coding field content (`judge_free_text.py`, 280 assessment pairs); (b) coder of the economics subsample (13 papers, structured output/tool_use, parallel fan-out, 0 errors); (c) adversarial reviewer in the Phase 11 consistency check (4-question test on 9 sections) and executor of narrative editing for chapters 3-9 (fan-out).

## 3. Human gates (human-in-the-loop)

- **Scope freeze (Phase 00):** RQ1-5, H1-H5, conceptual/empirical gaps and 5 scope dimensions - explicit dated author acceptance, 8 checkboxes.
- **D1-D4 (JES v2 adaptation):** D1 - recoding an economics subsample from the existing corpus (YES); D2 - worked example (NO, excessive complication); D3 - PL as base language, EN build derivative; D4 - second coder is a GPT model (not ChatGPT as a brand, but the latest version from the family, fixed and frozen at the start of coding).
- **Decision B2:** the "verifiability + cross-model check with human adjudication" variant instead of classic intercoder reliability with a second human coder (unavailable).
- **Cross-model discrepancy adjudications:** Variant A (Claude's threshold as canon) for the "GPT conservatism" pattern; 4 sub-decisions for the "definitional boundary" pattern (including the only actual data change - 2 Podejscie_eksploracja fields); review of 20 "different interpretation" items with the decision canon=Claude's code.
- **Prose acceptance:** content woven into sections 3.6 and 8.6 (PROZA_rozdz_3_8.md) requires author acceptance before being pasted into the manuscript.
- **Choice of second model and platform:** author's decision on the GPT family, version, execution account (outside the Claude Science account).
- **Sending and submission:** decision on venue (JES special issue vs. standard track), sending the proposal to editors, decision on revising the SSRN file on the day of JES submission, arXiv/Research Exchange upload - all on the author's side, the agent has no access to external accounts.
- **Editorial reservations (Phase 10):** 4 author interventions (table format as text not image, bibliography completeness, figure caption style, PL terminology + hyphens/dashes) - each forced a separate round of corrections and harness verification.

## 4. Limits and honesty

The AI may have erred at several points documented explicitly in the logs: a merge error skipped 32 arXiv records (fixed and **recorded as an explicit correction trace**, not masked); the layer label from the first matching rule turned out to be a poor proxy for corpus coverage (replaced with an independent metric after the venue decision); the LLM semantic judge over-interpreted bracketed qualifiers as content, generating false discrepancies (detected and corrected - **judge as a sieve, not a verdict**, final judgment belongs to the author). Cross-model validation yielded Cohen's kappa in the range 0.35-0.87 (after V1 adjudication): low agreement on inherently interpretive fields (Podejscie_eksploracja, Transferowalnosc) was treated as a signal directing human attention, not as evidence of error. Of the 215 identified discrepancies (42 dictionary-based + 138 partial + 35 divergent in free-text fields; canonical after V1 adjudication), only 2 fields required an actual data change - a result deemed healthy (the mechanism works as a sieve, not as a sham audit). Each of the 448 citations in the primary coding was verified programmatically against the source text; test data used to validate the agreement script was deleted immediately after the test so it would not end up in the results as fabrication. The baseline limitations remain explicit: substitution of Scopus/WoS/PubMed with OpenAlex+arXiv+Crossref, full-text coding depth of 41 out of 582 corpus papers, and the status of the FSAIM methodology stated explicitly as **provisional** - checked on a single instance (this article), not empirically compared with alternatives.
